idtransform 
       dx' dy' idtransform dx dy
dx' dy' matrix idtransform dx dy

With no matrix operand, idtransform (inverse delta transform) transforms the device space distance vector (dx', dy') by the inverse of CTM to produce the corresponding distance vector (dx, dy) in user space.

If the matrix operand is supplied, idtransform transforms the distance vector by the inverse of matrix rather than by the inverse of CTM.

A delta transformation is similar to a normal transformation, but the translation components (tx and ty) of the transformation matrix are not used, making the distance vectors be positionless in both user space and device space. idtransform is the inverse of dtransform. It is useful for determining how distances map from device space to user space.
